EXPERIENCE

As an intern at Movia, I helped build and maintain GPS production systems central to the performance of their mobile billboard systems used in advertising campaigns across North America.

At Helium, I designed a testing rig for the full-scale model of a Vertical Takeoff and Landing aircraft, and helped build the prototype that was displayed in the 2020 GoFly competition in San Francisco.

I spearheaded in the research, design, and simulation of the fuel and oxidizer tanks for a bi-propellant rocket engine. I also presented my team's findings to a team of engineers from the Canadian Space Agency as well as aerospace engineering professors from Ryerson University.

ACADEMIC PROJECTS

Design of a Hockey Helmet Impact Tester

I supervised the design analysis process for simulating 3000 hits on each side of a hockey helmet within an 8 hour workday. I also performed a risk assessment analysis to introduce failsafe measures to the electro-pneumatic system, leading to a safer design overall.

Design of a Bicycle Vibration Absorber

I calculated the parameters critical to vibration absorption of the motion of a moving bicycle, and aided in the design of the absorber system that could be reliably attached to a real bicycle.

Ball-Beam Control System

I modeled a ball-beam system in Simulink, and designed the PID system to stabilize the motion of a ball moving on a beam.

Design of a Coal-Geothermal Powerplant

I worked in a team to design a geothermal and fuel-based powerplant for a hypothetical population of 40,000 people, using theromydynamic analyses to determine energy needs as well as the optimal balance between efficiency, enviornmental sustainability, and cost-effectiveness.

Design of a Leaf Management System

I researched and aided in the design of a "leaf management system", where emphasis was placed on creating a product that would improve the general physical, mental, environmental, and social well-being of individuals in the Greater Toronto Area responsible for collecting leaves that have fallen onto their properties in urban and suburban areas, as well as create more livable communities.

Reverse Engineering a Geriatric Assistive Device

I worked in a team to reverse engineer a geriatric assistive device and came up implemented an improved design that avoids common pitfalls experienced by the elderly when using these types of devices.
